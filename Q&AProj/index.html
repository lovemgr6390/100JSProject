General Question and Answer

Question One would be who is lord Krishna?

Krishna is a major deity in Hinduism, revered as the eighth avatar of Vishnu. Known for his roles in the epic Mahabharata and the Bhagavad Gita, he is celebrated for his teachings on dharma, devotion, and righteousness. His stories encompass his childhood, youth, and divine wisdom.

Question two would be who is lord Shiva?

Shiva, a principal deity in Hinduism, is part of the Holy Trinity (Trimurti) alongside Brahma and Vishnu. Known as the destroyer and transformer, Shiva embodies both ascetic and householder aspects. Revered for his powers of destruction and regeneration, he is depicted with a third eye and the river Ganga flowing from his hair.

Question three would be who is Brahma?

Brahma is one of the principal deities in Hinduism, known as the creator god within the Holy Trinity (Trimurti) alongside Vishnu and Shiva. He is credited with the creation of the universe and all living beings. Depicted with four faces, each reciting one of the Vedas, Brahma symbolizes knowledge and creation.
